Complex Grammar Patterns

~는 동안

Used to express "while" doing something

저는 공부하는 동안 음악을 들어요.
jeoneun gongbu haneun dongan eumageul deureoyo
I listen to music while studying.

~기 때문에

Used to express reason ("because")

비가 오기 때문에 우산을 써요.
biga ogi ttaemune usaneul sseoyo
I use an umbrella because it's raining.

~려고

Used to express intention ("in order to")

책을 읽으려고 도서관에 갔어요.
chaegeul ilgeuryeogo doseogwane gasseoyo
I went to the library to read a book.

~(으)면서

Used to express "while simultaneously" doing something

저는 TV를 보면서 저녁을 먹어요.
jeoneun tibeureul bomyeonseo jeonyeogeul meogeoyo
I eat dinner while watching TV.

Sentence Structure

Korean follows a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) structure, which is different from English's SVO.

저는 사과를 먹어요.
jeoneun sagwareul meogeoyo
[I] [apple] [eat]
I eat an apple.
우리는 영화를 봅니다.
urineun yeonghwareul bomnida
[We] [movie] [watch]
We watch a movie.
학생이 도서관에 있어요.
haksaengi doseogwane isseoyo
[Student] [library] [is/exists]
The student is in the library.
나는 친구에게 선물을 줬어요.
naneun chinguege seonmureul jwosseoyo
[I] [to friend] [present] [gave]
I gave a present to my friend.
어머니가 저녁을 준비합니다.
eomeoniga jeonyeogeul junbihamnida
[Mother] [dinner] [prepares]
My mother prepares dinner.

Idiomatic Expressions

눈이 높다
nun-i nopda
Eyes are high
To have high standards
입이 무겁다
ibi mugeopda
Mouth is heavy
To be discreet, able to keep secrets
손이 크다
son-i keuda
Hands are big
To be generous/spend a lot
귀가 얇다
gwiga yalbda
Ears are thin
To be easily influenced by others
머리가 좋다
meoriga jota
Head is good
To be smart/clever

Cultural Context

Honorifics

Korean has complex honorific systems that reflect social hierarchy and relationships.

반말 (informal): 먹어
banmal              meogeo
Eat (informal)
존댓말 (formal): 드세요
jondaenmal      deuseyo
Eat (formal)

Age Importance

Age influences language, relationships, and hierarchy in Korean society.

형 (older brother for males)
hyeong
Older brother (for males)
오빠 (older brother for females)
oppa
Older brother (for females)

Bow Greetings

Bowing is an important greeting and sign of respect in Korean culture.

안녕하세요 (bowing slightly)
annyeonghaseyo
Hello (formal greeting)

Seollal (Korean New Year)

Traditional holiday with rituals, special foods and family gatherings.

새해 복 많이 받으세요
saehae bok mani badeuseyo
Happy New Year

Kimchi & Food Sharing

Sharing food and eating together is important in Korean culture, especially with kimchi.

김치를 나눠 먹어요
gimchireul nanwo meogeoyo
We share kimchi when we eat.

Grammar Quiz

Which particle indicates the topic of a sentence?

Correct answer: 는/은

What does the ending ~고 싶다 express?

Correct answer: Desire/Wish

Choose the correct honorific verb for "to eat":

Correct answer: 드시다

What does "손이 크다" mean?

Correct answer: To be generous/spend a lot

What is the meaning of "비가 오기 때문에 우산을 써요"?

Correct answer: I use an umbrella because it's raining
